# Color Palettes by Cultural Identity (Hierarchical Color)

## Color Hierarchy (The 60-30-10 Rule)
Within each aesthetic embassy, the palette applies this hierarchy to safeguard the usability of complex interfaces (searches and galleries):
1. **Base Color / Background (60%):** Usually fine nuances of white or extremely light grays. Ensures the photographic gallery does not compete with the background.
2. **Surface Color (30%):** Highlighted elements, background cards, and filter zones (sidebars).
3. **Accent Color (10%):** Subtle micro-interactions, highly recognizable but low-impact primary buttons (CTAs), fine separators.

---

## The 7 National Palettes

### 1. Netherlands (Order & Light)
- **Base (Bg):** Ice White (`#FFFFFF`)
- **Surface:** Fog Gray or extremely pale Silver (`#F4F6F8`)
- **Primary Text:** Deep Urban Charcoal (`#111822`)
- **Accent (CTAs):** Dark Canal Blue (`#1C355E`) with minuscule hints of Dutch Crimson inherited from its flag and naval history.

### 2. France (Heritage & Quiet Elegance)
- **Base (Bg):** Haussmann Cream (`#FAF9F6`)
- **Surface:** Slate Gray or Light Taupe (`#F0EEE9`)
- **Primary Text:** Softened Black, almost Anthracite (`#1A1A1A`)
- **Accent (CTAs):** Classic Ink Blue (`#2C3539`) or heavily muted Bordeaux Wine, representing the tactile luxury of French history.

### 3. Italy (Warmth & Material Soul)
- **Base (Bg):** Tuscan Ivory (`#FDFBF7`)
- **Surface:** Warm Sand (`#F5EFE6`)
- **Primary Text:** Very Dark Espresso Brown (`#2B1E16`)
- **Accent (CTAs):** Clay Terracotta Red (`#8E4A23`), a direct homage to the central material of its historical architecture.

### 4. Spain (Luminous Open Living)
- **Base (Bg):** Whitewashed / Alabaster White (`#FFFCF9`)
- **Surface:** Heavily Diluted Sun-baked Clay (`#F6EDDF`)
- **Primary Text:** Volcanic Gray / Dark Olive (`#2D3227`)
- **Accent (CTAs):** Dry Olive Green (`#4A5D23`) with punctual bursts of Ochre Yellow (sunlight and flag influence).

### 5. Portugal (Atlantic Calm)
- **Base (Bg):** Salt White / Cotton (`#FBFDFB`)
- **Surface:** Pale Sage Green / Ocean Gray (`#EEF2F5`)
- **Primary Text:** Atlantic Slate (`#303841`)
- **Accent (CTAs):** Antique Azulejo Blue (`#1D4E89`), directly tied to its national iconographic heritage.

### 6. Germany (Clarity & Precision)
- **Base (Bg):** Minimalist Off-White (`#F5F5F5`)
- **Surface:** Mineral Gray / Clear Steel (`#E8EBEB`)
- **Primary Text:** Pure Technical Black (`#000000`) - The only country that supports maximum contrast.
- **Accent (CTAs):** Dark Forest Green (Schwarzwald) (`#113322`), denoting precision, sustainable tech, and natural rigor.

### 7. Belgium (Timeless Character)
- **Base (Bg):** Warm Ivory (`#F5F4F0`)
- **Surface:** Grayish Clay (`#E6E4DD`)
- **Primary Text:** Deepened Sepia (`#2E2A27`)
- **Accent (CTAs):** Midnight Blue / Dark Burnt Brick (`#4A2E2B`). Evoking fine mystery, intact urban heritage, and masterful sobriety.
