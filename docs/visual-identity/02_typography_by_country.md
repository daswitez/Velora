# Adaptive Typography by Country (Cultural Typography)

## Foundation of the Duality
VELORA's typographic system masters the ambiguity between pure functionality (searches, data, filters) and emotional connection (heritage, aesthetics, context).

- **The Anchor (Base UI Sans-Serif):** Never changes. *Inter*, *Geist*, or another ultra-legible Neo-Grotesque font is implemented for all pure UI elements (buttons, pricing, navigation). This ensures mathematical usability is never compromised.
- **The Expression (Culture Display):** Drastically changes per country. This is the protagonist font (Hero, Property Titles, Editorial Titles), meticulously selected based on geography, flag, and architectural culture.

## Typographical Identity Mapping (Display)

### 1. Netherlands (Países Bajos)
*Style:* **Geometric Modernism / Neo-Grotesque (e.g., Helvetica Now Display or PP Neue Montreal)**
*Justification:* Dutch design (De Stijl) values purism, crystalline clarity, and mathematical order. Zero serifs, sharp edges, high visual technology, and vast spaces between blocks, reflecting its exact urbanism and mastery over water.

### 2. France
*Style:* **High Contrast Classical Serif (e.g., Bodoni Moda or Ogg)**
*Justification:* Represents Parisian high fashion, the weight of intellectual history, and indisputable refinement. The dramatic variation between the thin and thick stems of the font mimics the elegance of historic Haussmann facades and museum arches.

### 3. Italy
*Style:* **Warm Humanist Serif or Flared Serif (e.g., Cormorant Garamond or Canela)**
*Justification:* A typography featuring warm, almost hand-carved curves, harking back to the Renaissance era. It invites touching. It holds "soul" and organic textures that perfectly align with stone, terracotta, and southern light.

### 4. Spain
*Style:* **Expressive, Curvilinear, and "Soft" Serif (e.g., Recoleta or GT Alpina)**
*Justification:* Mediterranean design is open, welcoming, and solar. Far from being structured and rigid like the German counterpart, a vibrant typography with playful flared ends breathes an extroverted and warm attitude.

### 5. Portugal
*Style:* **Soft, Poetic, and Highly Legible Serif (e.g., Lora or Newsreader)**
*Justification:* A reminiscence of craftsmanship, the *azulejo*, and Atlantic calm. Slightly nostalgic, highly peaceful, and serene to read, steering completely free of loud cries for attention.

### 6. Germany
*Style:* **Industrial Sans-Serif or Mono-Dynamic (e.g., DIN Pro or Instrument Sans)**
*Justification:* Pure German engineering efficiency. Technical rigor, absolute precision, and a Bauhaus/Industrial focus. This font screams structural trust, functionality, and long-term quality.

### 7. Belgium
*Style:* **Timeless, Dark, and Discreet Serif (e.g., Playfair Display or Domaine Display)**
*Justification:* Evokes hidden luxury, an intellectual discretion ("dark academia" mood) aligned with its burnt brick interiors and elegant, somber aesthetics. An unmistakable stately bearing.
