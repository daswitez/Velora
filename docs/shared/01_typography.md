# Mother Typography (Typography)

## Overview
The typographic system of VELORA Living is the pillar of the "Silent Premium" aesthetic. It is designed to evoke the authority of a luxury editorial publication (an architecture magazine) combined with the impeccable legibility of a contemporary digital interface.

## Typographic Selection

### 1. Editorial Typography (Serif)
*Primary use: Large format headlines, section openings, collection names, and pull quotes.*
- **Personality:** Classic, refined, heavily influenced by European heritage (e.g., *Playfair Display*, *GT Sectra*, or *Canela*).
- **Treatment:** Usually utilized in lighter weights (Light/Regular). Requires excellent *kerning* and benefits heavily from generous display sizes.

### 2. Functional Typography (Sans-Serif)
*Primary use: Body text, navigation, property metadata (sqm, price, filters), buttons, and micro-copy.*
- **Personality:** Neutral, geometric, and extremely clear (e.g., *Inter*, *Roobert*, *Helvetica Now*).
- **Treatment:** Used across varied weights, but thin weights with open tracking (letter spacing) truly stand out for capitalized subheadings and labels, granting an urbane and technical tone.

## Pairing Rules
- **High Contrast:** The contrast between the precise, technical Sans-Serif text blocks and the organic, heritage-driven Serif headline is what generates the "contemporary European" aesthetic. Pairing multiple *display* fonts together is strictly prohibited.
- **Micro-Typography:** Real estate specifications (numbers, prices) must use tabular figures within the Sans-Serif font to align perfectly in comparative views and detailed property listings.
