# Tone of Voice 

## Overview
The VELORA Living platform demands that the entire interface speaks like an expert European curator. The content, both on the main landing (Diplomatic Headquarters) and the local portals, obeys the same corporate tone manual.

## Principles of the Editorial Tone

### 1. Sophistication Without Arrogance (Silent Premium)
- Luxury does not name itself (we never define things as "incredible", "super luxurious", "dirt cheap"). Value is assumed and presented.
- The voice speaks with absolute certainty while maintaining an observant, calm, and polite demeanor.

### 2. Culturally and Self-Aware
- We do not sell bricks; we contextualize the home. Value is placed on explaining the impact of *natural light*, the *original material*, the *history of the street*, and *proximity to the ecosystem* (cafés, museums, galleries, office hubs).
- **Bad Example:** *"Central, cheap apartment, 3 bedrooms. Buy now, it's a bargain!"*
- **Good Example:** *"A luminous residence of classical proportions. Located on the city's cultural axis, offering walkable living and inner calm."*

### 3. Assisted International Precision
- The interface does not speak just to fill space, nor does it commit the error of overloaded text. Copies throughout the site must be immaculate, brief, but highly impactful ("punchy and measured").
- When Artificial Intelligence intervenes (Smart Search), its descriptions must feel objective, pointing directly at tangible reasons *why* a home is recommended, rather than sounding like a sterile robot.

## The Common Message
Every person, regardless of the country page they enter, will read the same underlying promise from VELORA:  
*"This brand understands Europe, comprehends the context in which I wish to position myself, and curates a selection that rises to my life aspirations."*
